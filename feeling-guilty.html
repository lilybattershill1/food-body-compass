Feeling guilty about what or how I ate

When eating has left you with shame, regret, or a sense of having done something wrong.

What's going on

Food guilt often arrives quickly and feels very convincing. But guilt is a learned response — it comes from rules you've internalised about how you're supposed to eat.1 The fact that you're here, pausing to reflect, means you're already questioning those rules.

Try this now
1

Name it without judgement: "I'm feeling guilty right now." Saying it out loud reduces its grip.

2

Remind yourself: eating is not a moral act. You didn't do anything wrong.

3

Get curious rather than critical — whose rules are behind this guilt? Where did they come from?

4

Work through the guided journal below to understand the guilt and start to loosen its hold.
