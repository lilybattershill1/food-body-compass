Not feeling good in my body

When your body feels uncomfortable, critical, or hard to be in.

What's going on

This might show up as a heavy or disconnected feeling about your body — perhaps after looking in a mirror, trying on clothes, or just waking up. It's a hard place to be, and you don't have to fix it right now.

Try this now
1

Pause. Place a hand on your chest — you don't have to do anything yet.

2

Name it: "I'm having a hard body image moment." Labelling reduces its grip.1

3

Shift attention to what your body can do, feel, or sense right now.2

4

Try one of the guided audios below.

Sources
1Lieberman et al. (2007). Putting feelings into words: affect labeling disrupts amygdala activity in response to affective stimuli. Psychological Science, 18(5), 421–428. PubMed ↗
2Alleva et al. (2015). Expand Your Horizon: a programme that improves body image and reduces self-objectification by training women to focus on body functionality. Body Image, 15, 81–89. PubMed ↗
