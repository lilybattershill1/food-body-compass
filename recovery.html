🌱 Moving forward
after a binge

The episode is over. What you do now is what matters most.

This page is a guide, not a worksheet — use your journal to write.

Work through each section at your own pace. You don't have to do it all at once. The most important thing right now is that you're here, choosing to take care of yourself.

💡

A few things worth remembering

Restriction will make it worse

Skipping your next meal to "compensate" is one of the most reliable ways to trigger another binge.1 Eating normally after a binge is not giving in — it's recovery.

The shame spiral is not helpful

Self-criticism feels like it should do something — but it doesn't prevent future episodes. In fact, shame tends to increase them.2 Kindness is not a reward you have to earn.

This doesn't undo your progress

One episode doesn't erase the work you've done. Recovery is not a straight line and a binge is not a relapse into square one. You are still moving forward.

Your body is not damaged

Binges are uncomfortable, but your body is resilient. Drink some water, rest if you need to, and trust that it will settle.

🍽️

My next meal promise

Decide now what your next meal will be. It doesn't have to be perfect — it just has to be regular and kind.

In your journal, write:
My next meal will be…
I will eat it at approximately…
One thing I'll do to make it feel okay (e.g. eat sitting down, put it on a plate)…
🌿

One self-care choice

Choose one thing for yourself in the next hour that isn't food-related.

◦ Have a shower or bath
◦ Make a warm drink and sit quietly
◦ Go outside for 10 minutes
◦ Put on something comfortable
◦ Call or message someone you trust
◦ Listen to music or a podcast
◦ Lie down with a blanket
◦ Do something with your hands
In your journal, write:
My self-care choice right now is…
I'll do it…

Say something kind to yourself

You don't have to believe it fully. Use one of these, adapt it, or write your own.

"This was hard, and I got through it. I'm still here."
"I am allowed to struggle. Struggling doesn't make me broken."
"I would never speak to someone I love the way I'm speaking to myself right now."
"I don't have to be okay yet. I just have to take the next small step."
"Bingeing is something that happened to me — it is not who I am."
"I am learning. Every time I come back to myself, that is recovery."
In your journal, write your own:
Right now, I want to say to myself…
📓

Reflections (when you're ready)

Come back to these when you feel calm — not as analysis, but as understanding.

What was I feeling before the binge started?
Was there a specific trigger, or did it build gradually?
What was I hoping the binge would give me or take away?
Is there anything I might do differently next time I feel that way?
What do I want to remember from this moment — right now, after?
