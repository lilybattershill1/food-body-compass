Feeling the urge to restrict or skip meals

When eating feels like something to avoid, earn, or control.

What's going on

Even a tiny bit of restriction can feel like safety or even self-care — but it almost always increases the biological and psychological drive to binge later.1 Recognising this urge early is super powerful. It is very common to feel the urge to restrict after a binge in particular, but this normally just maintains the cycle.

Try this now
1

Notice without acting: "I notice I want to skip eating, change how I'm eating or cut back right now."

2

Ask: is this physical (not hungry) or emotional (wanting to feel in control or safe)?

3

Try to eat something — even if even something small counts as nourishment and care, and shows your brain that food is available (likely to reduce likelihood of next binge).

4

Ask yourself gently: what is this urge trying to protect me from? Sit with whatever comes up. Perhaps take out your journal and reflect on this question.

Sources
1Polivy & Herman (1985). Dieting and binging: a causal analysis. American Psychologist, 40(2), 193–201. PubMed ↗
