Step 2 of 5

What body image actually is

Body image isn't a mirror. It's a mental picture — and like all pictures, it's shaped by what you've been told to see. Diet culture, social media, and years of comparison wire the brain to notice perceived flaws and ignore everything else.

Research shows that body image is more strongly linked to mood, stress, and how safe you feel than to how your body actually looks. On a hard day, the same body can feel completely different to live in.

That means this feeling is real — but it's not objective fact. There is a gap between the thought "my body looks wrong" and the truth of what your body is.
