Step 2 of 5

Where guilt comes from

Guilt is a learned response. It often comes from rules — spoken or unspoken — that you've internalised about how you're "supposed" to eat. These rules didn't come from your body. They came from culture, diet messages, other people's fears, and a world that treats food as a moral issue.

But here's the truth: eating is not a moral issue. You didn't do anything wrong. You don't have to earn food, or punish yourself for needing it. Your worth is not determined by what you ate today.
